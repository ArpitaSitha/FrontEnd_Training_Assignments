Learn to Skydive


The thrill of a lifetime!



Accelerated Freefall (AFF) Course

The most common route to becoming a licensed skydiver in Australia.



Your first skydives will be with two instructors holding onto you in freefall, helping you remain in control and giving you signals reminding you what to do.

You’re not alone once you’re under parachute! Radio communication & ground signals are used to help guide you down the rest of the way.

AFF is a guided, step-by-step course that teaches you all the skills you need to be able to jump totally solo.

This normally takes 7-10 jumps depending on the course style & your progression.

Why Tandem?

Tandem skydiving is an introduction to the sport of skydiving. The Tandem Student wears a harness that connects to the Tandem Instructor who wears the parachute.

Together the tandem pair jump out of a plane usually between 10,000 to 15,000 feet above the ground. They freefall for up to one minute and then the Tandem Instructor opens the parachute and steers it back to the ground.

You can tandem skydive in Australia over spectacular beaches, outback panoramas, tropical rainforest and everything in between!

It's the ultimate, must-do experience for anyone with a sense of adventure.

Course Progress




Jumping with 2 Instructors

You’re not alone once you’re under parachute!

Your first skydives will be with two instructors holding onto you in freefall, helping you remain in control and giving you signals reminding you what to do.

Radio communication & ground signals are used to help guide you down the rest of the way.


Jumping with 1 Instructor

Learn to do a mid-air 360, and even a back flip!

As you become more competent in the air, you'll go with one instructor & they'll let go of you when you're ready. They’ll still be right there giving you any signals you need. You’ll learn how to do a mid-air 360, and even a back flip!


Jumping Totally Solo!

Ready to apply for your first skydiving license!

Once competent, the final jump on your Learn to Skydive course will be totally solo!

After you have completed your AFF course you’ll have the skills to jump solo. Once you have 10 additional solo freefall jumps you’ll be ready to apply for your first skydiving license.

Your ‘A-license’ will be recognised all over the world.
